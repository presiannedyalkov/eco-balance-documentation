# Growth & operations

How the project runs day to day, and how it grows. Growth happens in stages, each opened by evidence the last one worked, not by a date. Day-to-day work has to stay light enough for two part-time people.

## Phases and gates

- **Start small (prove it):** one modest site; get plantings established and learn. *Gate:* plantings surviving, basic systems working, funding and energy to continue.
- **Establish:** consolidate, form the entity, build partnerships, settle a monitoring routine. *Gate:* stable operations and reliable funding.
- **Expand:** more area or a second site. *Gate:* a proven model, the capacity to run more, and the money for it.
- **Institutionalise (optional):** a larger operation with some staff and research output. *Gate:* sustained funding and real demand.

Expanding on excitement rather than evidence is how small projects overreach, and balancing growth with conservation is hard even at national scale ([growth vs biodiversity, Africa](research/bookmarks/Climate%20Change/1742557794206.md), *news*). When a gate is met there is still a choice of direction — deepen in place, expand moderately, or scale up — and none is automatically right; it depends on capacity, funding, and the life you want.

## Operating rhythm

The work repeats on natural cycles; a simple calendar keeps it manageable.

- **Seasonal:** planting in the local window, watering through the dry months, maintenance, seed collection.
- **Monitoring:** survival checks and surveys on a fixed schedule.
- **Admin:** bookkeeping and a budget check each month.
- **Communication:** regular updates to supporters and partners.

Two part-time people cannot do everything at once. Match effort to the season, lean on volunteers for the heavy days, and decide in advance what gets dropped in a crunch — burnout is a bigger threat than most external risks. Tie decisions to the [governance](../organization/governance.md) tiers: routine work on the spot, bigger commitments wait.
