# Legal Framework

**← Back to [[00_Eco_Balance_Hub|Project Hub]]**

## Overview

This document outlines the legal structure and registration process for establishing Eco Balance as an NGO in Germany, focusing on environmental restoration activities in Southern Europe.

---

## Recommended Legal Structure

### gGmbH (Non-Profit Limited Liability Company)

The **gemeinnützige Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung (gGmbH)** is the recommended legal structure for Eco Balance. This combines:

- Limited liability protection of a GmbH
- Tax benefits of non-profit status
- Flexibility for international operations
- Professional operational framework

**Key Advantages:**
- Clear separation of liability
- Recognized structure for larger operations
- Can engage in some commercial activities within non-profit framework
- Suitable for receiving grants and donations
- Professional credibility with partners and funders

## Establishment Process

### Step 1: Define Company Purpose

**Articles of Association must include:**
- Clear charitable purpose: Environmental restoration in Southern Europe
- Specific focus on holistic ecosystem restoration (soil, water, biodiversity, community)
- Geographic scope and target regions (specifically Southern Europe initially)
- Commitment to using profits exclusively for charitable purposes
- Alignment with recognized non-profit activities under German tax law
- Explicit statement that all activities serve the public benefit

### Step 2: Choose Organization Name

**Requirements:**
- Indicates charitable purpose
- Not duplicating existing registered organizations
- Compliant with German naming regulations
- Available for registration

**Check availability:** Federal Ministry of Justice and Consumer Protection (BMJV) website

### Step 3: Draft Articles of Association (Satzung)

**Must include:**

**Basic Information:**
- Full organization name
- Registered office location in Germany
- Charitable purpose statement

**Governance Structure:**
- Board composition and responsibilities
- Managing director appointment process
- Decision-making procedures
- Term limits and succession planning

**Membership (if applicable):**
- Criteria for membership
- Rights and obligations
- Admission and expulsion procedures

**Financial Management:**
- Sources and use of funds
- Financial reporting requirements
- Auditing procedures
- Non-profit asset protection clauses

### Step 4: Raise Share Capital

**Requirements:**
- Minimum share capital: €25,000 for GmbH structure
- Must be deposited before registration
- Can be contributed by founders or initial investors

### Step 5: Appoint Leadership

**Required Positions:**
- At least one Managing Director (Geschäftsführer)
- Shareholders (no minimum number required)
- Optional: Advisory Board or Supervisory Board

### Step 6: Notarization

**Process:**
- Have Articles of Association officially recorded by German notary
- All founding shareholders must be present or represented
- Identity verification required
- Notarization fee applies

### Step 7: Commercial Register

**Registration with Handelsregister:**
- Submit notarized documents
- Include all required founder information
- Pay registration fees
- Obtain company registration number

### Step 8: Tax Registration

**Submit to local tax office (Finanzamt):**
- Completed tax registration questionnaire (Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung)
- Notarized Articles of Association
- Extract from Commercial Register
- Opening balance sheet
- Proof of charitable purpose

### Step 9: Obtain Non-Profit Status

**Gemeinnützigkeit Application:**
- Apply for provisional non-profit status
- Demonstrate exclusive charitable purpose
- Provide evidence of planned activities
- Final status granted retroactively after review period

**Tax Benefits Include:**
- Tax-deductible donations for supporters
- Exemption from corporate income tax
- Partial VAT exemptions
- Lower property and trade tax rates

## Detailed Legal Form Comparison

### Option 1: Verein (Small Association)

**Structure:**
- Small, non-profit association
- Membership-based structure
- Informal governance

**Best for:** Very small, local initiatives

**Not recommended for Eco Balance** due to limited operational capacity

### Option 2: Registered Association (eingetragener Verein - e.V.)

**Pros:**
- Simpler to establish
- No share capital requirement
- Lower administrative burden
- Traditional NGO structure

**Cons:**
- Less suitable for commercial activities
- May appear less professional for larger operations
- More limitations on governance flexibility

**Best for:** Smaller, volunteer-based initiatives

### Option 3: Foundation (Stiftung)

**Pros:**
- Permanent legal structure
- Suitable for long-term endowments
- High credibility
- Asset protection

**Cons:**
- High initial capital requirement (typically €50,000+)
- Complex establishment process
- Less operational flexibility
- More regulatory oversight

**Best for:** Large-scale projects with significant initial funding

### Option 4: gGmbH (Recommended)

**Structure:**
- Non-profit limited liability company
- Can engage in commercial activities to achieve charitable goals
- Professional corporate structure
- Limited liability protection

**Advantages for Eco Balance:**
- Flexibility for international operations
- Professional credibility with major funders
- Can generate revenue through eco-tourism and energy sales
- Strong legal protection for founders and directors
- Scalable structure for growth

**Best for:** Professional NGOs with significant operations, international scope, and revenue generation

### Decision Factors

Choose gGmbH if:
- Planning substantial operations and budget
- Need professional credibility
- Want limited liability protection
- Plan to engage in commercial activities (within limits)
- Seeking significant grant funding and partnerships

## EU and International Considerations

### EU VAT Registration

**When required:**
- Cross-border commercial activities
- Revenue above threshold in target countries
- Purchase of goods/services in other EU countries

**Process:**
- Obtain EU VAT identification number
- Register with EUCFC (European Public Register of Controlled Foreign Companies)
- Comply with transparency portal requirements

### Host Country Requirements

**For operations in Southern Europe (e.g., Portugal, Spain, Italy):**

**Land Purchase:**
- Research foreign ownership restrictions
- Comply with agricultural land regulations
- Understand environmental protection laws
- Obtain necessary permits for restoration activities

**Local Registration:**
- May need to register as foreign organization
- Comply with local environmental regulations
- Obtain work permits for staff if required
- Register for local taxes if conducting taxable activities

**Environmental Permits:**
- Water use permits
- Construction permits for buildings and infrastructure
- Environmental impact assessments
- Protected species permits if applicable

## Ongoing Compliance Requirements

### Annual Obligations

**German Tax Office:**
- Annual financial statements
- Tax return (even if exempt)
- Non-profit status documentation
- Activity reports demonstrating charitable purpose

**Commercial Register:**
- Update any changes to directors, address, or articles
- Annual confirmations as required
- Maintain current registration

**Financial Auditing:**
- Required when revenue exceeds certain thresholds
- Must be conducted by certified auditor
- Results submitted to tax office

### Record Keeping

**Must maintain:**
- Complete financial records (7-10 years)
- Documentation of all activities
- Donor records and acknowledgments
- Board meeting minutes
- Employment records
- Project documentation

### Transparency Requirements

**For donors and public:**
- Annual reports on activities and finances
- Public access to articles of association
- Regular updates on project progress
- Financial transparency for major donors

## Risk Management and Legal Protection

### Liability Protection

**GmbH structure provides:**
- Limited liability for shareholders
- Protection of personal assets
- Clear separation of organizational and personal finances
- Professional indemnity options available

### Insurance Requirements

**Essential coverage:**
- General liability insurance
- Professional indemnity insurance
- Directors and officers insurance
- Property insurance for facilities and equipment
- Employer's liability insurance (if hiring staff)
- Environmental liability insurance for restoration work

### Compliance Monitoring

**Regular reviews of:**
- Tax law changes affecting non-profits
- Environmental regulations in target countries
- Labor law compliance
- Data protection (GDPR) requirements
- Transparency and reporting standards

## Professional Advisors

### Essential Consultations

**Tax Advisor (Steuerberater):**
- Specializing in non-profit organizations
- Experience with international NGOs
- Can assist with:
  - Non-profit status application
  - Annual tax compliance
  - Financial structuring
  - Cross-border taxation issues

**Legal Counsel (Rechtsanwalt):**
- Experience in NGO/non-profit law
- Knowledge of environmental law
- Can assist with:
  - Articles of association drafting
  - Contract review
  - International operations
  - Liability protection

**Notary (Notar):**
- Required for official documentation
- Fixed fees by law
- Local to registration location
