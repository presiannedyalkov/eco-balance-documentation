## Executive Summary

File 51 contains 50+ quantitative KPI targets across ecological, social, financial, and operational categories. The most critical assumption is **measurement feasibility**: Can a bootstrap 1-2 person team afford and execute complex scientific measurements (microbial biomass, functional diversity indices, economic multipliers) while also running operations? ## Analysis Notes

**Strengths of KPI System**:
- Comprehensive coverage (ecological, social, financial, operational)
- Mix of leading (activity) and lagging (outcome) indicators
- Transparency commitment with public reporting
- Red flag thresholds for course correction

**Risks**:
- **Measurement complexity overwhelms small team** - 50+ KPIs is enterprise-level tracking for a 1-2 person startup
- **Cost of credibility not budgeted** - third-party verification, lab testing, audits could cost €15-25k/year
- **Baseline assumption unaddressed** - percentage targets meaningless without baseline, but baseline collection timing/cost unclear
- **Capacity assumption** - expects founders to be restoration ecologists + fundraisers + community organizers + data scientists + researchers

**Recommendation**:
Create a **phased KPI rollout**:
- **Year 1 Minimal Set** (10 KPIs): Trees planted, survival rate, land area, revenue, expenses, volunteers, website traffic, grants submitted, species count, canopy cover (all observable/countable, no lab work)
- **Year 2 Intermediate Set** (25 KPIs): Add soil/water testing (partner with university), carbon estimation, community surveys, financial audits
- **Year 3 Full Set** (50+ KPIs): Add complex scientific measurements (microbial biomass, functional diversity) once team/budget can support it

This approach builds credibility progressively without overwhelming bootstrap capacity or budget.
