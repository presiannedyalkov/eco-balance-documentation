## Top 3 Questions for User

### 1. CRITICAL: Risk Likelihood Validation
**What methodology was used to assign risk likelihood ratings (High/Medium/Low)?** Are these based on:
- Historical data from similar restoration projects?
- Expert panel assessment?
- Subjective team judgment?
- No specific methodology?

Without validated likelihoods, the entire risk prioritization and mitigation resource allocation may be misguided.

### 2. CRITICAL: Funding Source Confirmation
**How much personal savings is currently available, and have friends/family committed specific amounts?** The document lists "personal savings insufficient" and "friends/family funding falls short" as risk scenarios, but doesn't confirm:
- Current personal savings amount
- Whether friends/family have been approached
- What amounts (if any) have been committed
- Whether €20-30k target is realistic given actual available sources

### 3. CRITICAL: Mitigation Evidence
**Which mitigation strategies are proven effective versus theoretical?** For each major mitigation strategy, need to know:
- Has this been tested in similar restoration projects?
- What was the measured risk reduction?
- What is the cost/effort required?
- Which mitigations are highest priority given limited Year 1 resources?

Without this, resources may be wasted on ineffective mitigations while actual high-impact strategies are missed.

## Recommendations for Risk Document Improvement

### 1. Add Methodology Section
Document how risk likelihoods and impacts were determined. Cite data sources or expert input.

### 2. Validate Mitigation Effectiveness
For each mitigation strategy, cite evidence from similar projects or mark as "unproven - requires testing."

### 3. Quantify Where Possible
Replace qualitative ratings with quantitative probabilities where data exists (e.g., "30% chance based on X similar projects").

### 4. Add Risk Interdependencies
Map how risks compound (e.g., funding shortfall + land acquisition delay = project impossible).

### 5. Funding Source Reality Check
Cross-reference with Business Model document to confirm funding availability assumptions.

### 6. Separate Proven vs. Assumed
Mark which assumptions are validated (with evidence) versus still requiring validation.
