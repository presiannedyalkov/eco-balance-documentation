  - Assess any structures (safety, condition, legal)
- [ ] **Neighborhood Due Diligence**:
  - Talk to neighbors (relations, any conflicts?)
  - Check for planned developments nearby
  - Assess community attitude toward restoration
  - Understand local agricultural practices (chemicals, runoff)

## 7. Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid

### Pitfall 1: Falling in Love Too Soon
**Problem**: Emotionally committing to first property, overlooking flaws
**Warning Signs**: Skipping other site visits, ignoring red flags, overpaying
**Prevention**: Visit minimum 5 properties, use objective scorecard, sleep on decisions
**Recovery**: Walk away if due diligence reveals issues, accept sunk costs of research

### Pitfall 2: Inadequate Water Assessment
**Problem**: Underestimating water needs or development costs
**Warning Signs**: "Rain will be enough", vague water sources, no water testing
**Prevention**: Assess water critically, estimate development costs (wells ‚Ç¨2-5k+), ensure year-round availability
**Recovery**: Budget for water development, adjust restoration plan, worst case find new property

### Pitfall 3: Hidden Access Issues
**Problem**: Access not legally secured or impassable in wet season
**Warning Signs**: Informal access, no written easement, rough roads not tested in rain
**Prevention**: Verify legal access, visit in different seasons/weather, ensure year-round usability
**Recovery**: Negotiate access easement, improve roads, or reconsider property

### Pitfall 4: Overpaying Due to Limited Options
**Problem**: Paying above fair market value because few properties available
**Warning Signs**: Accepting asking price immediately, not researching comps, desperation
**Prevention**: Research comparable sales, get multiple options, be patient, know walk-away price
**Recovery**: Accept slight overpay if property truly perfect, or restart search

### Pitfall 5: Unclear Property Boundaries
**Problem**: Buying land with disputed or unclear boundaries
**Warning Signs**: No survey, "approximate" size, neighbor disputes
**Prevention**: Get professional survey, verify in property registry, walk boundaries with owner
**Recovery**: Renegotiate price, resolve disputes pre-purchase, or walk away

### Pitfall 6: Ignoring Soil Quality
**Problem**: Land too degraded or contaminated to restore affordably
**Warning Signs**: Skipping soil testing, assuming soil is fine, visible severe erosion
**Prevention**: Test soil (texture, pH, contamination if concerns), assess realistically
**Recovery**: Adjust restoration approach (more soil building), or find different property

### Pitfall 7: Lease Terms Too Short or Restrictive
**Problem**: Lease doesn't allow enough time or flexibility for restoration
**Warning Signs**: 1-2 year lease, "no modifications" clauses, easy termination
**Prevention**: Negotiate 5+ year lease, ensure planting/modification rights, renewal options
**Recovery**: Renegotiate terms, add protections, or choose purchase instead
