## Strategic Analysis Methodology

### Phase 1: Document Review
- Read all strategic documents (Files 01-12, 20-21, 30-32, 40-45)
- Extract all stated assumptions
- Identify implicit assumptions
- Map dependency chains

### Phase 2: Assumption Challenge
- For each assumption: "What if this is wrong?"
- Research base rates and benchmarks
- Calculate probability of assumption failure
- Assess impact if assumption fails

### Phase 3: Blind Spot Hunting
- What's not mentioned but should be?
- What stakeholders are overlooked?
- What seasonal/regulatory factors ignored?
- What resources assumed without verification?

### Phase 4: Scenario Development
- Best case: Everything works (5% probability?)
- Realistic case: Typical delays (70% probability?)
- Worst case: Major failures (25% probability?)
- Plan for realistic and worst cases

### Phase 5: Feasibility Testing
- Compare timelines to benchmarks
- Validate budgets against actual costs
- Assess team capacity vs workload
- Test critical path dependencies

### Phase 6: Report Generation
- Strategic Soundness Score calculation
- Prioritized challenges and questions
- Scenario analysis with probabilities
- Recommended strategic adjustments

## Notes

Strategic Advisor role is to strengthen strategy through constructive challenge, not to discourage ambitious goals. The goal is to:
1. **Surface blind spots** before they become problems
2. **Validate assumptions** with evidence
3. **Prepare contingencies** for likely obstacles
4. **Ensure realistic planning** that honors both vision and constraints

Key principle: "Strong plans survive strong challenges. Weak plans reveal themselves early when challenged, preventing wasted effort."

First strategic review should focus on Year 1 Bootstrap phase as it's the most immediate and critical for project launch success.
