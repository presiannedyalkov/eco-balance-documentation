## Cross-Cutting Themes

### Theme 1: Specificity Deficit

**Pattern**: Documents present options, frameworks, and ranges but stakeholders need decisions, numbers, and commitments.

**Examples**:
- Legal structure: "e.V., AssociaÃ§Ã£o, or other" â†’ Need: Decision
- Budget: "â‚¬15-30K" â†’ Need: Specific ask with breakdown
- Timeline: "6 scenarios" â†’ Need: Base case with triggers
- Projections: "â‚¬50K consulting" â†’ Need: "10 workshops Ã— 20 people Ã— â‚¬250"

**Impact**: Creates perception of unreadiness, indecision, or lack of rigor

**Fix Strategy**: Choose base case, show alternatives as contingencies

### Theme 2: Stakeholder Inversion

**Pattern**: Project needs dominate; stakeholder needs secondary.

**Examples**:
- Community success = participation in project (not community-defined outcomes)
- Partnership = access to expertise (not mutual value creation)
- Volunteer = labor hours (not satisfaction and growth)
- Funder = money source (not impact partner)

**Impact**: Stakeholders feel used not partnered

**Fix Strategy**: Reframe every section: "What's in it for them?"

### Theme 3: Technical-First Framing

**Pattern**: Emphasis on methods, technology, processes over human benefits and relationships.

**Examples**:
- Vision: "Implement systems" not "Transform lives"
- Solution: "Technology integration" before "Community empowerment"
- Success: Ecological metrics emphasized over social metrics
- Case studies: Technical achievements over community transformation

**Impact**: Alienates non-technical stakeholders, feels impersonal

**Fix Strategy**: Lead with human impact, follow with technical approach

### Theme 4: Pre-Operational Gaps

**Pattern**: Critical operational prerequisites undefined or TBD.

**Examples**:
- No legal entity (blocks funding, partnerships, compliance)
- No land (blocks all operations)
- Team capacity << scope (feasibility concerns)
- Regulatory status unknown (risk of blocking issues)

**Impact**: Stakeholders question readiness and timeline realism

**Fix Strategy**: Ruthlessly prioritize prerequisites, sequence dependencies
