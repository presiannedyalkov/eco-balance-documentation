## Overview

Reforestation is a critical pillar of ecosystem restoration. The goal is not just to plant trees, but to **restore an entire ecosystem** by considering the needs of local wildlife, soil health, and overall ecological balance.

**Key Principle:** Work with nature, not against it. Select native species adapted to local conditions and use proven planting methods that maximize survival and long-term success.

---

## Strategic Context

**From [[../../20_Restoration_Methodology|Restoration Methodology]]:**
- **Year 1 Target:** 200-500 trees (1 hectare pilot)
- **Future Scaling:** 1,000-2,000 trees (Years 2-3 - 5-10 hectares)

**Calculation basis:**
- 1 hectare = ~200-500 trees (depending on spacing and species mix)
- 5 hectares = ~1,000-2,500 trees
- 10 hectares = ~2,000-5,000 trees

- Focus on native, drought-tolerant, fire-resistant species
- Use Miyawaki method and assisted natural regeneration
- Create diverse age and species structure (no monocultures)

**Integration with Other Pillars:**
- **Soil Health:** Healthy soil is essential for tree survival → [[soil_restoration/00_Soil_Restoration_Overview|Soil Restoration]]
- **Water Management:** Adequate water during establishment → [[water_management/00_Water_Management_Overview|Water Management]]
- **Biodiversity:** Trees create habitat for wildlife → [[biodiversity/00_Biodiversity_Overview|Biodiversity Conservation]]
- **Community:** Local participation in planting activities → [[community_engagement/00_Community_Engagement_Overview|Community Engagement]]

## Implementation Process

### Phase 1: Planning & Preparation (Months 1-3)

1. **[[01_Identify_Native_Species|Identify Native Species]]**
   - Research native trees and shrubs
   - Consult botanists and ecologists
   - Consider climate adaptation and ecosystem roles
   - *Duration: 2-4 weeks*

2. **[[02_Source_Seedlings|Source Seedlings]]**
   - Find reputable nurseries or suppliers
   - Ensure health and disease-free stock
   - Plan timing for optimal planting season
   - *Duration: 4-6 weeks (including lead time)*

3. **[[03_Prepare_the_Land|Prepare the Land]]**
   - Clear debris and assess soil conditions
   - Plan planting layout and spacing
   - Improve soil fertility if needed
   - *Duration: 2-3 weeks*

### Phase 2: Implementation (Months 3-4)

4. **[[04_Plant_the_Seedlings|Plant the Seedlings]]**
   - Follow proper planting techniques
   - Ensure adequate spacing and depth
   - Water thoroughly after planting
   - *Duration: 2-4 weeks depending on scale*

### Phase 3: Monitoring & Management (Ongoing)

5. **[[05_Monitor_Growth|Monitor Growth]]**
   - Regular site visits and health checks
   - Track survival rates and growth metrics
   - Replace dead or diseased trees promptly
   - *Frequency: Monthly (Year 1), Quarterly thereafter*

6. **[[06_Long_Term_Management|Long-Term Management]]**
   - Pruning and thinning as needed
   - Pest, disease, and fire protection
   - Adaptive management based on results
   - *Duration: 5-10+ years*

## Success Metrics

**Survival Rates:**
- Target: >70% survival after Year 1
- Target: >60% survival after Year 3

**Growth Metrics:**
- Monitor height and diameter annually
- Track canopy cover development
- Measure carbon sequestration

**Ecosystem Function:**
- Native wildlife utilization of habitat
- Soil improvement under tree canopy
- Water retention and microclimate effects
