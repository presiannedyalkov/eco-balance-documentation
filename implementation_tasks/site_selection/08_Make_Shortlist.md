# Make a Shortlist

‚Üê Back to [[README|Implementation Tasks]] | [[00_Site_Selection_Overview|Site Selection Overview]]

---

## üìã Purpose

After conducting thorough research, assessments, and site visits, it's time to synthesize all information and create a shortlist of the most suitable locations for your restoration project. This critical step requires balancing multiple factors - ecological potential, practical considerations, financial constraints, and strategic alignment with your mission.

---

## üéØ Objectives

- Synthesize all research and assessment data
- Apply consistent evaluation criteria across all sites
- Create objective scoring system for comparison
- Identify top 2-3 candidate sites
- Document rationale for shortlist selections
- Prepare detailed site profiles for final decision
- Identify next steps for shortlisted sites

## üìù Step-by-Step Shortlisting Process

### Step 1: Compile All Site Data

**Gather Information:**
- Desk research findings ‚Üí [[01_Identify_Potential_Locations|Initial Research]]
- Land condition assessments ‚Üí [[02_Evaluate_Land_Condition|Land Evaluation]]

**Create Master Spreadsheet:**
- List all candidate sites
- Compile key data points for each
- Include costs and timelines
- Note opportunities and challenges
- Record stakeholder input
- Document regulatory status

### Step 2: Define Decision Criteria

**Essential Criteria (Must-Haves):**
These are non-negotiable - sites failing any of these are eliminated:

- [ ] **Legal Access:** Can legally acquire or lease the land
- [ ] **Budget Fit:** Acquisition costs within available budget
- [ ] **Size Appropriate:** 1 hectare for Bootstrap pilot (v2.0 Model)
- [ ] **Restoration Viable:** Degradation severe enough to justify intervention, but not impossible
- [ ] **Regulatory Feasible:** Can obtain necessary permits within reasonable timeline
- [ ] **Safety:** Safe for staff and visitors to access and work
- [ ] **Geographic:** Located in target region (Southern Europe)

**Sites failing essential criteria are eliminated immediately.**

**Bootstrap Model Site Size:**
- Year 1-3: 1 hectare pilot site (prove the model)
- Post-pilot: If successful, consider 5-10 ha replication sites
- Focus: Validation at small scale, not immediate large-scale operation
- Budget: 1 ha site fits within ‚Ç¨20-30k Year 1 budget

### Step 4: Score All Sites

**Apply Scoring System:**

Complete the scoring matrix for each candidate site:

**Scoring Guidelines (1-5 scale):**
- **5 = Excellent:** Exceptional, major advantage
- **4 = Good:** Above average, positive factor
- **3 = Adequate:** Acceptable, no major concern
- **2 = Poor:** Below standard, significant concern
- **1 = Very Poor:** Major problem, potential deal-breaker

**Be Honest and Objective:**
- Use consistent standards across sites
- Base scores on evidence, not emotions
- Involve multiple team members for objectivity
- Document rationale for each score
- Flag any uncertainties or missing data

**Example Site Comparison:**

| Site | Ecological | Practical | Financial | Strategic | TOTAL |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Site A: Sierra Valle | 29/35 | 22/30 | 14/20 | 11/15 | **76/100** |
| Site B: Monte Verde | 32/35 | 18/30 | 16/20 | 13/15 | **79/100** |
| Site C: Rio Seco | 26/35 | 25/30 | 18/20 | 10/15 | **79/100** |
| Site D: Pinar Alto | 30/35 | 20/30 | 10/20 | 12/15 | **72/100** |

### Step 5: Analyze Scores and Identify Patterns

**Look Beyond Total Scores:**

- **High Scorers:** Obviously strong candidates
- **Close Scores:** Need deeper comparative analysis
- **Category Strengths:** Site A ecological, Site C practical/financial
- **Fatal Flaws:** Any category scoring below 50% of possible points?
- **Risk Assessment:** Low scores identify major risks ‚Üí [[../../50_Risk_Assessment|Risk Assessment]]

**Consider Trade-offs:**

**Scenario 1: Perfect Ecology vs. Practical Challenges**
- Site scores 32/35 ecological but only 15/30 practical
- Question: Can practical challenges be overcome?
- Decision: If budgets allow infrastructure investment, maybe worth it

**Scenario 2: Easy Operations vs. Limited Impact**
- Site scores 28/30 practical but 22/35 ecological
- Question: Is restoration impact sufficient to justify?
- Decision: Depends on mission priorities and demonstration value

**Scenario 3: Financial Constraints**
- Multiple high-scoring sites, but acquisition costs vary widely
- Question: Is higher cost justified by higher potential?
- Decision: Factor in long-term financial sustainability

### Step 6: Conduct Sensitivity Analysis

**Test Your Assumptions:**

**"What If" Scenarios:**
- What if budget is 20% less than expected?
- What if permit timelines double?
- What if key landowner refuses to sell?
- What if climate predictions worsen?
- What if community opposition emerges?

**Adjust Scores:**
- Which sites remain viable under stress scenarios?
- Which are most resilient to uncertainty?
- Which have backup options or flexibility?

**Risk-Adjusted Scoring:**
- Reduce scores for high-uncertainty factors
- Increase scores for sites with clear path forward
- Consider probability of success, not just potential

### Step 7: Create Shortlist (2-3 Sites)

**Selection Criteria:**

**Ideal Shortlist:**
- Top 2-3 scoring sites that:
  - Pass all essential criteria
  - Score above 70/100 overall
  - No category below 50% of possible points
  - Clear path to acquisition
  - Acceptable risk profile
  - Aligned with organizational capacity

**Diversity in Shortlist:**
- Consider including different site types for comparison
- High ecological potential vs. high practical feasibility
- Different geographic locations or contexts
- Backup options if primary choice falls through

**Example Shortlist:**

**Primary Candidate: Monte Verde (79/100)**
- Strengths: Exceptional ecological potential, strong community support
- Weaknesses: Moderate accessibility challenges
- Rationale: Highest overall score, best balance of factors
- Next Steps: Begin detailed due diligence, start negotiations

**Secondary Candidate: Rio Seco (79/100)**
- Strengths: Excellent practical factors, lowest operating costs
- Weaknesses: Slightly lower ecological restoration potential
- Rationale: Tied score, easier operations, good backup
- Next Steps: Maintain contact, keep as viable alternative

**Tertiary Candidate: Sierra Valle (76/100)**
- Strengths: Very good ecology, accessible location
- Weaknesses: Higher acquisition cost, regulatory uncertainties
- Rationale: Solid option if top two don't work out
- Next Steps: Continue relationship, monitor regulatory changes

### Step 9: Validate with Stakeholders

**Internal Team Review:**
- Present shortlist to team members
- Discuss rationale and scoring
- Address concerns and questions
- Build consensus around top choices
- Assign roles for next steps

**Advisory Board Input:**
- Share profiles with advisors
- Seek expert opinions
- Leverage their experience

- Share shortlist with funders showing interest

**Community Soundings:**

**Benefits:**
- Transparency for funders and stakeholders

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Analysis Paralysis**
- Problem: Too much data, can't make decision, keep researching
- Solution: Set decision deadline. Use scoring system to force prioritization. Remember: no site is perfect. Make best decision with available information.

**Challenge 2: Emotional Favorites**
- Problem: Team has different favorite sites based on personal preferences
- Solution: Return to objective scoring. Discuss specific concerns. Use weighted criteria to resolve ties. Leader makes final call if needed with clear rationale.

**Challenge 3: Close Scores**
- Problem: Multiple sites score very similarly, no clear winner
- Solution: Good problem to have! Identify differentiating factors. Consider strategic timing (which is available now?). Visit top sites again. Choose based on secondary criteria or gut instinct of team leader.

**Challenge 4: Top Site Has Major Flaw**
- Problem: Highest scoring site has one serious weakness
- Solution: Assess if flaw is fixable. Calculate cost/timeline to address. Compare to next-best option. Sometimes second-best with no major flaws is better choice.

**Challenge 5: Stakeholder Disagreement**
- Problem: Funders, advisors, or team members strongly disagree on choice
- Solution: Facilitate structured discussion. Identify root causes of disagreement. Seek compromise or creative solution. Document concerns and how addressed. Leadership makes final call but explains clearly.

**Challenge 6: New Information Emerges**
- Problem: During shortlisting, learn something that changes assessment
- Solution: Update scores immediately. Reassess if it changes rankings. Don't ignore new data. Better to adjust now than after commitment.

**Challenge 7: Perfect Site Unattainable**
- Problem: Top scoring site landowner won't sell, or price unrealistic
- Solution: This is why you have 2-3 on shortlist! Move to next option. Don't chase unattainable. Stay mission-focused, not site-focused.

## üí° Tips & Best Practices

**Use Data, Not Emotions:**
- Sites can be beautiful but impractical
- Don't fall in love with any single site
- Scoring system keeps you honest
- Involve multiple perspectives

**Document Everything:**
- Future you will thank present you
- Funders want to see rationale
- Learning for next time
- Defensible decision-making

**Leave Backup Options:**
- Don't put all eggs in one basket
- Keep 2-3 sites warm
- Negotiations can fall through
- Flexibility is strategic advantage

**Set Deadlines:**
- Analysis can continue forever
- Make decision and move forward
- Perfect is enemy of good
- Time is finite resource

**Communicate Clearly:**
- Keep landowners informed
- Update stakeholders regularly
- Transparent about timeline
- Manage expectations

**Be Ready to Walk Away:**
- Some sites won't work
- Don't force bad fit
- Mission over specific location
- Other opportunities exist

**Trust the Process:**
- Systematic approach reduces risk
- Multiple perspectives improve decision
- Data-driven beats intuition alone
- But incorporate experience and gut feel

**Plan Next Steps:**
- Shortlist is beginning, not end
- Due diligence phase starts now
- Legal and technical assessments
- Financing and negotiations

**Stay Mission-Focused:**
- Remember why you're doing this
- Site is means, not end
- Environmental restoration is goal
- Choose site that serves mission best
