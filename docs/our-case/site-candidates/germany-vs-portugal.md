## Reading it

They come out almost level, which fits reality. There is no clearly correct answer here, only a trade-off.

Germany wins on water, fire safety, funding, and being home, with no move and no language barrier. It loses on cost and on how much land, and what kind of view, we can realistically get.

Portugal wins clearly on affordability and on getting real area with a view, and it fits "live the rest of life in nature" better. It loses on water, fire risk, and the upheaval of moving into another language.

## What would tip it

- **Toward Portugal:** a tight budget that still wants three to five hectares and a view, a firm willingness to relocate, and comfort designing for fire and drought.
- **Toward Germany:** wanting to start without uprooting, valuing water security and easy administration, and accepting one to two hectares at a higher price.
