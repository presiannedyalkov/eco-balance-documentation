## ðŸ‘¥ Governance Bodies

### Board of Directors

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members
- Target 5 members by Year 2
- Diverse expertise and backgrounds
- Independent majority (non-staff)

**Term:**
- 3-year terms
- Renewable once (max 6 years continuous)
- Staggered terms for continuity

**Meetings:**
- Quarterly minimum (4x/year)
- Special meetings as needed
- Virtual meetings allowed
- Quorum: 50% + 1 member

**Responsibilities:**
1. **Strategic Oversight**
   - Approve strategic plan
   - Monitor progress toward goals
   - Ensure mission alignment

2. **Financial Governance**
   - Approve annual budget
   - Review financial statements
   - Ensure fiscal responsibility
   - Approve major expenditures

3. **Executive Oversight**
   - Hire/evaluate Executive Director
   - Set compensation
   - Succession planning

4. **Risk Management**
   - Identify organizational risks
   - Ensure adequate insurance
   - Review risk mitigation strategies

5. **Policy Approval**
   - Adopt governing policies
   - Review and update as needed
   - Ensure compliance

**Committees:**
- **Finance Committee:** Budget, audit, investments
- **Governance Committee:** Board recruitment, policies
- **Programs Committee:** Impact and effectiveness

---

### Advisory Board

**Composition:**
- 5-7 volunteer experts
- Non-governing role (advisory only)
- Diverse expertise

**Expertise Areas:**
- Restoration ecology
- Sustainable finance
- Policy and governance
- Community development
- Technology/innovation
- Carbon markets
- Education/outreach

**Meetings:**
- Bi-annual (2x/year)
- Ad hoc consultations as needed

**Responsibilities:**
- Provide technical guidance
- Strategic counsel
- Network connections
- Credibility and endorsement
- No fiduciary duty

---

### Shareholders Meeting

**Composition:** All shareholders

**Frequency:** Annual general meeting

**Responsibilities:**
- Elect Board members
- Approve major changes (articles, dissolution)
- Receive annual report
- Major strategic decisions

## ðŸ“‹ Key Policies

### 1. Conflict of Interest Policy

**Purpose:** Prevent personal benefit from organizational decisions

**Requirements:**
- Annual disclosure by all Board and staff
- Recusal from conflicted decisions
- Documentation of conflicts
- Transparent processes

**Examples of Conflicts:**
- Personal financial interest in vendor
- Family member employed
- Board member on partner organization board
- Competing business interests

---

### 2. Financial Management Policy

**Principles:**
- Accurate record-keeping
- Budget discipline
- Dual oversight (checks and balances)
- Regular reporting

**Requirements:**
- Dual signatures for >â‚¬5,000
- Competitive bidding for >â‚¬10,000
- Documented approval for >â‚¬25,000
- Board approval for >â‚¬50,000

**Financial Controls:**
- Segregation of duties
- Regular reconciliation
- Expense documentation
- Audit trail

---

### 3. Fundraising Ethics Policy

**Principles:**
- Honesty in solicitation
- Donor privacy
- Gift acceptance standards
- Transparency in use of funds

**Requirements:**
- Accurate representation of need
- Clear communication of impact
- Donor recognition as requested
- Refund policy if misrepresentation

**Gift Acceptance:**
- Cash and securities: Always accepted
- Real estate: Requires due diligence
- In-kind: Must serve mission
- Restricted gifts: Must be feasible

---

### 4. Whistleblower Protection Policy

**Purpose:** Enable reporting of misconduct without retaliation

**Process:**
1. Multiple reporting channels (Board, external hotline)
2. Anonymous reporting allowed
3. Investigation procedures
4. Anti-retaliation provisions
5. Confidentiality protection

---

### 5. Document Retention Policy

**Purpose:** Maintain appropriate records, destroy appropriately

**Retention Periods:**
- **Permanent:** Articles, minutes, tax exemption, audits
- **7 years:** Financial records, tax returns, contracts
- **3 years:** Bank statements, expense reports
- **Until superseded:** Policies, job descriptions

**Destruction:** Secure shredding after retention period
