## Overview

**Eco Balance is a bootstrap ecological restoration project** founded by two committed individuals working to create a network of self-sustaining restoration sites across Europe. We're building a new model that combines:

- **Land restoration** using holistic ecological methods
- **Research collaboration** hosting scientists and students
- **Self-sustaining living** through tiny house homesteading
- **Network replication** establishing multiple connected sites over time
- **Permanent protection** ensuring restored land stays wild for generations

**We are not building a traditional NGO.** We are two people starting small, learning by doing, and creating a replicable model that others can adopt.

## Our Unique Approach

### 1. Bootstrap, Don't Fundraise-First

**Traditional NGO approach**: Raise €250-500k → Build organization → Hire staff → Start work

**Our approach**: Start small with own resources → Prove concept → Attract support → Scale sustainably

**Why**: We can start immediately, maintain full control, learn by doing, no pressure from large donors

### 2. Research Collaboration as Core Value

**Not just restoration** - We're creating **research platforms** where scientists can:
- Conduct long-term ecological studies
- Test restoration techniques
- Gather data for models and publications
- Access well-documented baseline conditions

**Benefits for us**:
- Scientific credibility
- Free/low-cost technical expertise
- Publications that attract more researchers
- Potential grant funding
- Continuous learning

**Benefits for researchers**:
- Field site access without cost
- Willing collaborators for measurements
- Long-term commitment (we live there!)
- Integrated restoration experiments

### 3. Permanent Land Protection

**Problem**: Restored land often gets developed or exploited again after restoration project ends.

**Our solution**:
- Legal entity (e.V. → Stiftung) owns land
- Conservation easements prevent future development
- Land protected "in perpetuity" (hundreds of years)
- Even when we move to next site, protection remains

**Mechanisms** (Germany):
- Grunddienstbarkeit (conservation easement)
- Stiftung (foundation) ownership
- Partnering with existing land trusts
- Legal covenants in land title

### 4. Self-Sustaining Living Infrastructure

**Integrated approach**: Tiny house homesteading + restoration work

**Practical benefits**:
- Low cost of living enables focus on restoration
- Live on-site for daily monitoring and care
- Demonstrate sustainable living practices
- Transferable to next stewards when we move on

**Components**:
- Tiny house (built or purchased: €15-40k)
- Off-grid systems (solar, water, composting)
- Permaculture integration (food, resilience)
- Internet connection (for remote work initially)

### 5. Network Model for Replication

**Not scaling one organization** - Creating a **replicable pattern**:

Each site:
- 2-5 hectares restored land
- 1-2 committed stewards living on-site
- Self-sustaining infrastructure
- Active research collaborations
- Permanent legal protection

Network benefits:
- Shared knowledge and best practices
- Mutual support (tools, labor, advice)
- Combined fundraising potential
- Greater ecological and social impact
- Model can spread without central control

**Handoff protocol** (when founders move to next site):
- Find and train new stewards (1-2 year overlap)
- Transfer operational knowledge
- Maintain legal protection (land stays with entity)
- New stewards become part of network
- Founders start next site with proven model

## Organizational Structure Evolution

### Year 1: Informal Start

**Legal**: None yet, operating as individuals
**Team**: 2 founders
**Governance**: Consensus between founders
**Finances**: Personal accounts, clear tracking

### Year 2: Entity Establishment

**Legal**: e.V. (Eingetragener Verein)
- 2 founders + 5 supporting members (7 total minimum)
- 3-person board (Vorstand): Founders + 1 trusted advisor
- Tax-exempt status (gemeinnützig) for donations
- Annual general assembly (mostly formality)

**Team**: Still 2 founders doing the work
**Governance**: Board decisions (usually consensus)
**Finances**: e.V. bank account, proper accounting

### Years 3-5: Operational Maturity

**Legal**: Still e.V., possibly transition land to Stiftung
**Team**: 2 founders + volunteer researchers + occasional helpers
**Governance**: Proven board, clear financial processes
**Finances**: Multiple revenue streams, founders draw salaries

### Years 6-10: Network Formation

**Legal**:
- e.V. or network coordination entity
- Stiftung holds land assets for permanent protection
- Each new site may replicate this structure or join existing entity

**Team**: Multiple sites, each with 1-2 stewards
**Governance**: Network coordination mechanism (annual gathering? Shared decision-making?)
**Finances**: Each site self-sustaining, some shared fundraising

## Risk Management

### What Could Go Wrong

**Personal risks**:
- Health issues or injury (only 2 people)
- Relationship conflict between founders
- Burnout from doing everything ourselves
- Income instability during transition to full-time

**Financial risks**:
- Donations don't materialize
- Land costs more than expected
- Can't maintain jobs while building project
- Underestimate operational costs

**Operational risks**:
- Restoration fails due to climate, pests, or errors
- Can't find research collaborators
- Legal/regulatory obstacles
- Community opposition to project

**Network risks**:
- Can't find suitable next stewards for handoff
- Sites lose focus after founders leave
- Network coordination becomes burdensome
- Model doesn't replicate as hoped

### How We'll Mitigate

**Personal**:
- Health insurance and emergency fund
- Clear communication and conflict resolution practices
- Regular breaks and community support
- Maintain remote work capability for income stability

**Financial**:
- Start small, scale based on actual funding
- Build reserves before quitting jobs
- Diversify income sources
- Conservative budgeting

**Operational**:
- Learn from established projects (case studies)
- Start with proven techniques
- Build research collaborations early
- Engage community from beginning

**Network**:
- Document everything for future stewards
- Build community around sites
- Keep model simple and replicable
- Accept that not every site will succeed
