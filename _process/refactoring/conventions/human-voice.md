# Write like a person, not a model

The point of the refactor is to escape generic AI prose. Every doc should read like it was written by someone who knows the subject and cares about getting it right. Use this as an editing pass.

## Cut these words and phrases

- Filler vocabulary: delve, seamless, robust, leverage, foster, harness, unlock, empower, navigate (figurative), embark, vibrant, rich tapestry, realm, testament, crucial, pivotal, vital, utilize (say "use").
- Hype: groundbreaking, transformative, game-changing, revolutionary, cutting-edge, world-class.
- Padding: "it's important to note," "it's worth noting," "needless to say," "in today's world," "at the end of the day."
- Throat-clearing transitions used cosmetically: "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally." Only use a transition when it's earned.
- Wrapper closers: "In conclusion," "In summary," "Overall."

## Avoid these structures

- The "from X to Y" opener.
- Sentences that trail off in an -ing clause ("…, highlighting the need for…", "…, revealing key insights").
- Three-part rhythms for effect (the rule of three, used over and over).
- Rhetorical questions planted to sound thoughtful ("But what does this mean in practice?").
- Manufactured balance ("while some argue X, others contend Y") where no real debate exists.
- Em dashes for drama. Use them rarely. A comma, period, or parentheses usually works better. (We were badly over-using them.)
- Identical paragraph shape throughout (topic sentence, evidence, mini-summary, repeat). Vary length and purpose.
- Bullet lists where flowing sentences would read better. Lists are for genuine enumerations and options.

## Aim for

- Commit to a view. Say what we think and why, instead of touring every position.
- Concrete over generic. Real places, numbers, species, costs — not "a dedicated team" or "a vibrant ecosystem."
- Varied rhythm. Mix short and long sentences. A fragment is fine when it lands.
- Plain words over impressive ones.
- Some unevenness. Not every section needs a tidy bow. Honest beats polished.

If a sentence could sit unchanged in any project's docs, it's too generic. Make it ours.
